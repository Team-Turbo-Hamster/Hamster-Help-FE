HAMSTER HELP

User guide

Thankyou for choosing Hamster Help!

Setting up

Your first step should be to register an account with the server. Click on the REGISTER button. This will redirect you to the registration page. You will be prompted for a Name, a Username, an Email Address and a Password, and an avatar image. There is also a switch to declare whether you are a tutor or a student. The Name will be what others will see you as and Username will be what you use to log into the system.



Enter your details on the form. If any of your entries are not complete, a message will appear on screen telling you what conditions your inputs have to fulfil. Once all checks are met, the Submit button will be enabled. Clicking on it will return you to the login screen.


Logging in
To log in, enter the Username and Password you supplied when you registered then press Submit.

Once you are logged in, you will be taken to the app’s Main Menu.

an icon in the top right corner will display your avatar - clicking on it will open the profile menu.  
-        ‘Toggle Theme(light/dark) will change the colour profile of the app between bright and dark to reduce eyestrain.

-        ‘My Profile’ will show you your profile page with your details and a list of tickets associated with your account, sorted into two tabs: Unresolved and Resolved.

-        ‘Log Out’ will return you to the login screen.

You can also click on ‘Hamster Help’ in the header bar to be returned to the Main Menu from anywhere in the app.

Student

If you are a student, you will have three options on the Main Menu:
-        ‘Create ticket’ - This takes you to the ticket creation form which you use to submit your issue to your tutors.
-        ‘Open Queue’ - this takes you to the Queue containing all currently active tickets. You can see where your ticket is in the queue, or read and comment on any other tickets in the queue (unless the ticket creator has chosen to set it ‘private’) if you have suggestions to add!

-        ‘Open Stack’ - this takes you to an archive of all previously resolved tickets. These can be sorted by keyword or user. See if someone else has had the same problem before, and how they resolved it.


Create Ticket
On the Ticket Creation page you will be prompted for the following information:
-        Title - this should be a short description of the problem, no more than 100 characters, which will appear on the front of the Ticket card.

-        Body - this should be a more detailed description of the problem you have, along with when and how it came about. This can be as long asi t needs to be - the text box will expand to fit.

-        Tags - you will be presented with a cloud of tags relevant to your field of study. Click on any you think are relevant to the problem you’re having, or type your own into the box below. We recommend between two and six tags.

-        Set as Private - this prevents other students from reading or attaching comments to your ticket (don’t worry, the tutors will still be able to see it)
-        Choose File - this is where you can attach screenshots or other supporting files to your ticket.

Once the title and body have been filled in, the Submit button can be used to add your ticket to the queue. While you’re waiting for a tutor to become available, why not  see if other students are waiting in the queue with similar issues, or research past solutions in the stack!







Queue


Clicking ‘Open Queue’ takes you to a list of all currently active (not yet resolved) Tickets in ‘card’ format, sorted with the oldest (next to be seen) at the top. The ticket Card will show you at a glance the avatar of the student who posted it, the title of the ticket, the number of comments it has and any tags added - as long as the ticket was not set to private when it was created.


Stack

Clicking ‘Open Stack’ from the main menu will take you to the Stack view, a list of tickets which have already been resolved. This functions much like the Queue does, but by default it sorts tickets with the youngest (most recently resolved) first.

        
Ticket View


Clicking on a Ticket Card in the Queue will display the ticket in full. If you have any insights on the issue your fellow student is having, you can add a comment at the bottom of the page which all students and tutors reading the ticket will see.

If you are the student who submitted the ticket, this is where you would go to cancel your request for help. A ‘Close Ticket’ button will be visible at the bottom of the page; clicking on it will remove your ticket from the queue.

Tag View

Clicking on one of the tags in a Ticket View will display a list of all other tickets which have been given that tag, sorted into two tabs: Unresolved and Resolved.


Consulting with a Tutor

When a tutor opens your ticket, you will be prompted with a popup asking you to join them in Chat. clicking OK on this box will take you to a modified Ticket View, showing the details of your ticket and a chatroom containing you and the tutor.

Tutor

Jump right in!

This button will jump you straight to the first ticket in your queue, usually the oldest unresolved ticket.

Open Queue

Clicking ‘Open Queue’ takes you to a list of all currently active (not yet resolved) Tickets in ‘card’ format, sorted with the oldest (next to be seen) at the top. The ticket Card will show you at a glance the avatar of the student who posted it, the title of the ticket, the number of comments it has and any tags added. Clicking on the card will take you to an expanded ticket View.

Open Stack

Clicking ‘Open Stack’ from the main menu will take you to the Stack view, a list of tickets which have already been resolved. This functions much like the Queue does, but by default it sorts tickets with the youngest (most recently resolved) first. Clicking on the card will take you to an expanded ticket view

Ticket View

Clicking on a Ticket Card in the Queue will display the full ticket details, along with any supporting files the student may have added. This will include any comments added by other students.

Once you have looked over the ticket to your satisfaction, click on the ‘Open Ticket’ button. This will open a private chatroom at the bottom of the ticket and send the student a popup asking them to join you in it.

When the student’s issue has been resolved, click the ‘Close Ticket’ button above the chat. You will be prompted to write a brief explanation of the solution you provided, then returned to the Queue view.

If you are viewing a ticket from the stack, i.e. one which is already resolved, the Ticket View will show the chat logs of the tutor’s conversation with the student, any notes the tutor left about how the issue was resolved, and a button to ‘Reopen Ticket’ which will set the Ticket back to ‘active’ and sort it back into the queue.
